# Somalia Financial Analysis – Detailed Insights

This document contains detailed insights derived from SQL analysis on Somalia's government financial dataset. 
Each section explains the observed results and possible reasons behind the patterns.

---

## 2.1: Average annual spending

**SQL Result Summary:**  
- The average annual spending from 2015–2022 shows a clear increase in 2022.  
- Previous years (2015–2021) show smaller fluctuations, with a gradual upward trend.

**Insight / Interpretation:**  
- 2022 has the highest average spending, indicating a surge in government financial activity.  
- Spending in previous years was more stable but gradually increasing.

**Possible Root Causes:**  
- Increased budget allocation for social programs, infrastructure, or security initiatives.  
- Possible receipt of additional international aid or funding in 2022.  
- Post-pandemic recovery programs or economic stimulus measures could have contributed.

**Patterns / Notes:**  
- There is a general upward trend over the years, showing gradual financial growth.  
- Spikes in certain years may reflect specific large projects or external funding injections.

---

## 2.2: Average yearly spending by ministry / chapter

**SQL Result Summary:**  
- 2022: Ministry of Labor & Social Affairs spent the most.  
- 2015–2021: Ministry of Defense consistently had the highest spending every year.  
- Other ministries fluctuate but generally have smaller spending totals.

**Insight / Interpretation:**  
- Defense dominates historical spending, reflecting ongoing operational and security needs.  
- In 2022, Labor & Social Affairs surpassed Defense, indicating a policy shift or focus on social programs.

**Possible Root Causes:**  
- Increased social programs, welfare support, and labor initiatives in 2022.  
- Defense high spending historically due to security and operational costs.  
- Budget reallocation or external funding could have affected ministry priorities.

**Patterns / Notes:**  
- Ministry spending patterns can indicate government priorities over time.  
- Sudden changes may correspond to national policy shifts or emergency programs.

---

## 2.3: Highest spending expense categories

**SQL Result Summary:**  
- Top 5 spending categories:  
  1. Social benefits  
  2. Social assistance  
  3. Transfers to non-residents  
  4. Transfers to other government units  
  5. Transfers to sub-national government  

**Insight / Interpretation:**  
- Social programs consume the largest portion of the budget.  
- Government also spends significantly on inter-governmental transfers and obligations to non-residents.

**Possible Root Causes:**  
- Prioritization of welfare and humanitarian programs.  
- Inter-governmental transfers required for decentralization or regional development.  
- International obligations and payments to non-residents.

**Patterns / Notes:**  
- Expense types reflect the government’s focus on social and inter-governmental responsibilities.  
- Some smaller categories may represent operational or administrative spending.

## 2.4: Spending on internationally funded projects

**SQL Result Summary:**  
- Top funded projects:  
  1. RCRF project  
  2. PFM project  
  3. SFF project  
- Remaining projects receive smaller allocations.

**Insight / Interpretation:**  
- RCRF, PFM, and SFF are strategic projects prioritized for international support.  
- These projects may have larger scopes or higher importance to national or donor objectives.

**Possible Root Causes:**  
- Donor funding and strategic importance drive allocations.  
- High funding may also indicate urgent national priorities or multi-year project commitments.

**Patterns / Notes:**  
- International funding is concentrated in a few top-priority projects.  
- Other projects receive smaller allocations, possibly due to limited scope or phased funding.

---

## Overall Observations

- Government spending shows a **gradual upward trend** over the years.  
- **Ministry-wise analysis** reveals shifts in government priorities, especially in 2022.  
- **Expense category analysis** highlights that social programs are the top priority.  
- **International projects** demonstrate targeted funding to high-impact initiatives.  

These insights provide a comprehensive understanding of Somalia’s financial management and can inform policy recommendations and decision-making.
